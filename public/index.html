FILE 01: The Post-War Partition

In 1945, the dust settled on a broken Germany. The Allies—the US, UK, France, and USSR—didn't just share a victory; they shared the land. Germany was split into four zones.

The Berlin Paradox: Berlin was the ultimate prize. Despite being 100 miles deep within the Soviet-controlled East, the city itself was split into four sectors. This created an island of democracy in a sea of communism.

FILE 02: Operation Little Vittles

When the Soviets blocked all land routes into West Berlin in 1948, the city faced starvation. The West responded with the Berlin Airlift.

Lieutenant Gail Halvorsen noticed the children of Berlin had nothing. He began dropping candy attached to tiny parachutes. He would wiggle his plane's wings so children knew the "Candy Bomber" had arrived.
